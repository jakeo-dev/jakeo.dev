festoon

verb, adorn (a place) with ribbons, garlands, or other decorations.

To festoon captures a whimsical feeling, while decorating is too formal and fancy. The president of the United States can decorate the White House with his golden ribbons, 12-inch bows, and sparkling white lights all he wants, but the American people will be festooning their cozy homes with madder poinsettias and evergreen garland.

fob

verb, deceitfully attempt to satisfy someone by making excuses or giving them something inferior.

As the popularity of words that negatively describe other people and their actions (gaslighting, gatekeeping, love-bombing, toxicity, whataboutism, ghosting, cancelling, victim-blaming) is increasing, why not add another to your expanding vocabulary? Call someone out for fobbing when they give you a toe in exchange for the leg you gave them.
